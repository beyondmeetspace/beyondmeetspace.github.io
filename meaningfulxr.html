MEANINGFUL XR

The Meaningful XR conference has roots in the Meaningful Play conference hosted at Michigan State University (MSU) since 2008, the SPARTIE Lab at MSU, and the NSF-funded Beyond Meet Space project team. The inaugural Meaningful XR Conference (MXR24), held on Stanford University campus in May 2024. We support remote attendance in a virtual world (in VR Chat) with a livestream of the physical conference room, with some physical attendees also in the virtual world via VR headset.

Meaningful XR 2025: Exploring the Future of XR Together

Meaningful XR 2025 took place May 22–23 at the UC Davis International Center and online, offering a hybrid experience that welcomes both in-person and virtual attendees. The conference built on the momentum of past events by uniting researchers, educators, students, and industry professionals to examine how XR (extended reality) technologies—VR, AR, and MR—can create meaningful impact across fields.

The program included an exciting lineup of keynote speakers from top institutions, sessions on emerging research, immersive demos, and social events.

Remote participation was supported via Zoom and VRChat. VRChat attendance occurred via headsets, desktops, or mobile devices, making the conference truly borderless.

MXR 2025 was made possible by an outstanding coalition of sponsors and organizing partners, including the SPARTIE Lab, SIUE, Stanford’s Virtual Human Interaction Lab, and more.

Meaningful XR 2024: A Resounding Success

The MXR24 conference brought together nearly 90 scholars, creators, and innovators from over 30 institutions for 2.5 days of vibrant presentations, rich conversations, and immersive social events—both in person and in VR. With a hybrid format that included Zoom, screen-sharing, and VRChat streaming, attendees engaged from across the globe, some even in headsets. The conference’s intimate yet dynamic scale created space for meaningful exchange while forging new connections in the XR research community.

Thanks to the support of generous sponsors, including Stanford, MSU, the Journal of Media Psychology, and the NSF, 18 students received travel scholarships to attend. MXR24 was powered by a dedicated leadership team and tireless volunteers whose collaboration made the event seamless and inspiring. With overwhelming enthusiasm from attendees, MXR is poised to become an annual tradition in the XR community.

Program findings · 2021–2025

Three findings that frame the work.

From a five-study program on virtual meetings, well-being, and equity in synchronous mediated interaction.

Finding 01 · Meta-analysis

Feeling trapped drives fatigue.

.33r

Across 38 studies, "feeling trapped" was the strongest predictor of videoconferencing fatigue — ahead of audio issues, camera problems, and meeting length.

Beyea et al., 2025 · Computers in Human Behavior Reports

Finding 02 · Survey

Avatar customization is an equity tool.

Equity
signal

Women and people of color place significantly higher value on avatar customization in virtual workplaces — reframing customization as infrastructure, not decoration.

Lim et al., 2024 · Computers in Human Behavior

Finding 03 · In-world study

Play reduces fatigue.

Play as
countermeasure

Gesture tools, stylized environments, and ambient virtual objects reduce fatigue and increase engagement — shifting virtual meetings from endurance to participation.

Foxman et al., DiGRA 2024
